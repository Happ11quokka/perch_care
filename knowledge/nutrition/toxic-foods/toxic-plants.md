# Toxic Plants

## Overview

Many common household and garden plants are toxic to companion birds. Birds are naturally curious and will chew on accessible plants during out-of-cage time, making plant toxicity a significant hazard. The toxic compounds vary by plant species and can affect different organ systems, from gastrointestinal irritation to cardiac failure. Bird owners should carefully evaluate all plants in their home and outdoor areas where birds have access.

## Highly Toxic Plants (Can Be Fatal)

| Plant | Toxic Compound | Primary Effects |
|-------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Avocado** (*Persea americana*) -- leaves, bark | Persin | Cardiac failure, respiratory distress |
| **Oleander** (*Nerium oleander*) | Cardiac glycosides (oleandrin) | Cardiac arrhythmia, death |
| **Lily of the Valley** (*Convallaria majalis*) | Cardiac glycosides (convallatoxin) | Cardiac arrhythmia, death |
| **Foxglove** (*Digitalis purpurea*) | Cardiac glycosides (digitoxin) | Cardiac arrhythmia, death |
| **Yew** (*Taxus* spp.) | Taxine alkaloids | Sudden cardiac arrest |
| **Castor bean** (*Ricinus communis*) | Ricin | Multi-organ failure |
| **Dieffenbachia** (*Dieffenbachia* spp.) | Calcium oxalate crystals | Oral swelling, airway obstruction |
| **Philodendron** (*Philodendron* spp.) | Calcium oxalate crystals | Oral irritation, GI distress |
| **Sago palm** (*Cycas revoluta*) | Cycasin | Liver failure |

## Moderately Toxic Plants

| Plant | Toxic Compound | Primary Effects |
|-------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Poinsettia** (*Euphorbia pulcherrima*) | Diterpene esters (latex) | GI irritation, skin irritation |
| **Daffodil** (*Narcissus* spp.) | Lycorine alkaloids | Vomiting, diarrhea, tremors |
| **Tulip** (*Tulipa* spp.) -- bulb | Tulipalin A and B | GI irritation |
| **Amaryllis** (*Amaryllis* spp.) | Lycorine alkaloids | GI distress, tremors |
| **Ivy** (*Hedera helix*) | Saponins, falcarinol | GI irritation, dermatitis |
| **Azalea/Rhododendron** (*Rhododendron* spp.) | Grayanotoxins | GI distress, cardiac effects |
| **Hydrangea** (*Hydrangea* spp.) | Cyanogenic glycosides | GI irritation, cyanide release |
| **Wisteria** (*Wisteria* spp.) | Lectin, wisterin glycoside | GI distress |
| **Morning Glory** (*Ipomoea* spp.) | Lysergic acid derivatives | Neurological effects |

## Mildly Toxic / Irritant Plants

- **Pothos** (*Epipremnum aureum*): Calcium oxalate crystals; oral irritation
- **Peace Lily** (*Spathiphyllum*): Calcium oxalate crystals; oral and GI irritation
- **Rubber plant** (*Ficus elastica*): Latex; GI irritation
- **Aloe vera**: Saponins; GI irritation (outer leaf; inner gel is less toxic)
- **Jade plant** (*Crassula ovata*): Unknown compound; GI distress

## Safe Plants for Bird Environments

The following plants are generally considered safe around companion birds:
- Spider plant (*Chlorophytum comosum*)
- Boston fern (*Nephrolepis exaltata*)
- African violet (*Saintpaulia* spp.)
- Bamboo palm (*Chamaedorea* spp.)
- Herbs: basil, parsley, dill, cilantro, mint, oregano, thyme
- Wheatgrass and cat grass
- Hibiscus (*Hibiscus rosa-sinensis*)
- Nasturtium (*Tropaeolum* spp.)
- Sunflower (*Helianthus annuus*)
- Marigold (*Calendula officinalis*)

**Always verify plant safety** before placing in a bird-accessible area. "Safe" plants should also be free of pesticides and chemical treatments.

## Clinical Signs of Plant Poisoning

Signs vary by the toxic compound involved:
- **GI irritation**: Vomiting, diarrhea, crop stasis, oral swelling
- **Cardiac toxicity**: Weakness, arrhythmia, sudden death
- **Neurological toxicity**: Tremors, seizures, ataxia, disorientation
- **Hepatotoxicity**: Lethargy, green/yellow discoloration of urates, anorexia
- **Oral/dermal irritation**: Swelling of tongue and oral tissues, skin redness

## Prevention

- Remove all toxic plants from areas accessible to birds.
- Research every houseplant before bringing it into a home with birds.
- During out-of-cage time, supervise and redirect birds away from plants.
- Do not assume "organic" or "pesticide-free" plants are safe -- the plant itself may be toxic.
- When in doubt, keep the plant out of reach or remove it entirely.
